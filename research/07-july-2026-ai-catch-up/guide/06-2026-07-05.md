### The Log is the Agent


The ActiveGraph paper proposes making an append-only event log the source of
truth for agent state and deriving deterministic projections from it. That
architecture enables replay, forks, lineage, and inspection across tool calls
instead of treating a mutable conversation as the whole agent. The paper was
submitted in May and qualifies here through July 5 discovery and discussion.
Its authors explicitly do not claim they demonstrated self-improvement.

HN participants compared the design with event sourcing and CQRS, questioning
its novelty while discussing deterministic replay, KV state, and external tool
state. That critique narrows the contribution: the value is an agent-specific
composition and implementation, not invention of append-only logs.

<div class="impact" markdown="1">

**Builder impact:** Long-running agents can gain reproducibility and auditability
by recording immutable events and rebuilding derived state, but external side
effects still need idempotency, snapshots, and explicit replay semantics.
