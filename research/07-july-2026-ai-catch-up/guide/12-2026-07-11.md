## What else mattered

### Terry Tao uses agents for inspectable mathematical applications

Tao reported porting roughly two dozen old Java applets to JavaScript in hours. The agent introduced one minor bug, found two older bugs, and then helped build a Minkowski-sum visualization that he had earlier abandoned because the interface work was too costly. His boundary condition matters: these were secondary visual aids with outputs an expert could inspect, not delegated mathematical proofs or irreversible production changes.

<div class="impact" markdown="1">

**Builder impact:** Coding agents are unusually effective when the deliverable is bounded, visual, reversible, and reviewed by someone who can recognize a plausible but wrong result.

</div>
